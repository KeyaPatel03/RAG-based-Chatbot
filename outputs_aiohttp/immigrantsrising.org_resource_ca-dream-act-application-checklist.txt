STEP 1: COMPLETE THE CA DREAM ACT APPLICATION TO GET HELP PAYING FOR COLLEGE!

Complete this checklist to ensure that you’ve completed all necessary steps in order to be considered for all state financial aid, including the Cal Grant and Middle-Class Scholarship.

• Understand how you meet AB 540 requirements. Beginning in January 2024, the AB 540 Affidavit is now embedded within the CADAA. The affidavit will be shared directly with all the colleges and universities you listed. It’s helpful to have unofficial transcripts and/or attendance records from all the schools you attended to accurately answer the Affidavit in the CADAA. For your financial aid to be released, you must meet these requirements. U visa holders and students with TPS are also eligible. T visa holders should apply through FAFSA.
• For your financial aid to be released, you must meet these requirements. U visa holders and students with TPS are also eligible. T visa holders should apply through FAFSA.
• If you are a new user, create a username and password for the CADAA. Save them somewhere safe, where you won’t forget, like a contact on your phone or a piece of paper you keep with other important documents.
• Gather the following information : Your and your parents’ 2024 tax information, untaxed income, bank statements, household size, number of people in the household attending college, etc. You will need these when answering questions related to you or your parent(s).
• If your 2024 taxes aren’t complete by the priority deadline, March 2* , indicate “Will file” and use estimated household earnings. After your or your parent’s 2024 taxes are filed, log in to update your application.
• If you are a non-tax filer, indicate “Will not file” and list all untaxed income for the household. Be aware of the IRS filing requirement as a non-tax filer.
• List all colleges and universities you applied to on the CA Dream Act Application. You can include up to 20 campuses. Be sure to list at least one California college or university to be considered for Cal Grants, which are state-based financial aid.
• Submit your CA Dream Act Application and review the confirmation page immediately for errors. Complete the application by March 2* , to meet the priority deadline. You can apply after this date, but many grants, such as the Cal Grant and the Middle Class Scholarship, will only consider students who meet the priority deadline. NOTE: Students attending community colleges can apply until September 2.

• Ensure your parent signs the application if required. Only one parent needs to sign the application. NOTE: Each time a change is made to the application, you must resubmit it. Always re-check the confirmation page for any outstanding items marked in red.

• Verify your GPA has been submitted to the CA Student Aid Commission before March 2 (or September 2 for community college students) at mygrantinfo.csac.ca.gov . If not , complete the student portion of the Cal Grant GPA Verification Form , then share it with your high school counselor or community college official to fill out their part. Once completed, your counselor or school official should email it to [email protected] with the subject “Cal Grant GPA Verification Form G-4.”
