WHY HISTORY MATTERS?

History is a discipline built on questioning, evidence, interpretation, and understanding how change happens. It gives you clarity about the world you live in, and how you can influence the future.

• You study real people, real decisions, and real consequences.
• You learn how ideas form, how institutions evolve.
• You learn how power, identity, and culture shape societies.

In this timely lecture, Dr. James Mestaz (Department of History and Politics) explores the historical relationship between imperial power, oil, and U.S. intervention in Venezuela. The talk examines how decades of energy interests, strategic concerns, and domestic political pressures created an entangled history that still shapes relations between the two countries today. In his chapter “Social Conflict in Late Antique Rome” in
A Companion to Rome (c. 400–c. 1050)
, edited by Caroline Goodson and Julia Hillner, Samuel Cohen examines patterns of social conflict in Rome from the fourth through the seventh centuries. His chapter focuses on food insecurity, urban entertainment, and divisions within the Roman church, particularly during contested episcopal elections, and traces how long-standing civic frameworks centered on the urban prefect were gradually supplanted by a Christian social order structured around the authority of the bishop. ALUMNI SHAPING THE FUTURE

My experience with the SSU History Journal helped me to serve as editor-in-chief of the Ohio State Journal on Dispute Resolution. My senior seminar course helped provide a foundation to succeed in labor and employment law.

The BA in history I earned at SSU has helped me in my career as a systems administrator and soon-to-be software developer by developing critical thinking skills and encouraging me to look at multiple viewpoints.

The scholarly guidance and mentorship I received in the SSU History Dept not only cemented my love for and dedication to the historical discipline, it provided me with a suite of valuable intellectual tools and professional skills.

The History Dept at SSU taught me the value of highlighting untold stories and including marginalized voices in interpretations of the past. While working for the National Park Service, I have the opportunity to do so every day.

In an era of “fake news” and increasingly myopic world views, history teaches us to carefully consider multiple viewpoints and the validity of our sources. I have used my skills as a historian to tell a wide variety of impactful stories.

Earning my BA in history at SSU has been incredibly useful in my job as an archivist, where I need to evaluate information, make informed decisions, do background research, and write reports to senior leaders and other stakeholders.

My history degree has been an advantage throughout my career. The research I had to do for my degree has helped me stand out when researching current economic affairs that may impact the bank.

Learning the stories of humanity’s resilience in the past inspires patience in the present and hope for the future. The professors in the SSU History Department excel at telling these stories.

SSU is dedicated to students and their scholarship. I found lifelong mentors that helped me become an educator. Studying history at SSU, you’ll find a community, not a department.

The History M.A. offered the freedom to further explore my academic interests, and the guidance to apply my course studies to a career in museum studies. The internships I participated in during the program have proved invaluable in my professional growth.

My graduate studies at the SSU history department enabled me to ultimately realize my professional objectives. Our seminar course acquianted me with the discipline of 'public history' which I pursued as my career.
