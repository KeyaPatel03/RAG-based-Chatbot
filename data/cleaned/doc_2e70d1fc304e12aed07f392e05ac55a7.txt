FINANCIAL AID FOR STUDENTS WITH DISABILITIES

KEY TAKEAWAYS

• Students with disabilities can access federal aid like Pell Grants by filing the FAFSA, which does not affect SSDI or SSI benefits.
• SSDI and SSI provide monthly benefits, and vocational rehabilitation benefits can cover the cost of education, training, and assistive technology.
• Specialized scholarships, such as Ruby’s Rainbow, offer awards of up to $10,000 for eligible students.
According to the National Center for Education Statistics,
students with disabilities
made up
over 20% of undergraduate students
in 2020. Data shows that
ADHD is the most common disability
, followed by other learning disabilities and autism spectrum disorder.

As a student with a disability, you can qualify for financial aid opportunities by filling out the Free Application for Federal Student Aid (FAFSA). Additionally, you may also qualify for non-educational aid like Social Security Disability Insurance (SSDI) and Supplemental Security Income (SSI) if you have proof from a medical professional, the Social Security Administration, or the U.S. Department of Veterans Affairs (VA).

Read on to learn how you may qualify for federal and social security financial aid and what scholarships you may be eligible for.

OPPORTUNITIES FOR FINANCIAL AID

As a student with a disability, you may be able to qualify for federal financial aid, social security income, and private scholarships.

FEDERAL FINANCIAL AID FOR STUDENTS WITH DISABILITIES

One option that helps many students with disabilities pay for college is federal financial aid, including the Pell Grant. All students must complete the FAFSA to receive federal financial assistance.

Pell Grants are income-based grants for students with financial need. These do not need to be repaid. You’ll receive a Student Aid Index (SAI) of how much you’re expected to pay for college. The
maximum Pell Grant
for the 2025-26 academic year is $7,395.

Students with SSDI or SSI are also welcome to submit a FAFSA form, and the Pell Grant does not impact your eligibility to receive disability compensation.

The
federal FAFSA deadline
is on June 30 of the school year for which you need aid. However, some states and schools award financial aid on a first-come, first-served basis. Therefore, it is important to submit your FAFSA as early as you can.

SOCIAL SECURITY DISABILITY INSURANCE (SSDI)

Social Security Disability Insurance
provides monthly benefits to individuals with disabilities and their eligible family members based on their work history and the payment of Social Security taxes. The benefit amount is determined by your length of employment and your earnings during that period.

SSDI itself does not pay for you to go to college; however, if you receive benefits, you may be eligible for vocational rehabilitation benefits, which can pay for postsecondary education, on-the-job training, assistive technology, and career counseling.

However, you may not be automatically eligible for SSDI. To qualify, you’ll need to
fulfill certain criteria
established by the Social Security Administration, which can include medical documentation from a medical professional or the VA.

SUPPLEMENTAL SECURITY INCOME (SSI)

Supplemental Security Income
provides financial assistance to older adults and/or those with disabilities. To qualify for SSI, you must be over the age of 65 or be any age with a disability or blindness. You must also possess limited income and resources to sustain yourself.

Many states provide additional support on top of federal benefits, including food assistance, healthcare services, and supplemental income payments. While students can work and receive SSI, the program does have limitations on earned income.

SCHOLARSHIPS FOR STUDENTS WITH DISABILITIES

Below, we compiled a list of just a few of the many specialized
scholarships for students with disabilities
. These are awards you don’t have to pay back, and they can reduce the out-of-pocket cost of earning a degree by thousands of dollars.

COCHLEAR ACADEMIC OR VOCATIONAL SCHOLARSHIPS

• Who Is Eligible: Cochlear Nucleus, Baha, or Osia recipients pursuing a technical, vocational, trade school, undergraduate, or graduate program
• Scholarship Amount: $2,000 annually for up to four years

RUBY’S RAINBOW COLLEGE SCHOLARSHIP

• Who Is Eligible: Students with Down syndrome who are at least 18 years old or will turn 18 by August 1 of the application year
• Scholarship Amount: $1,000-$10,000

JARED MONROE FOUNDATION SCHOLARSHIP

• Who Is Eligible: Undergraduate or graduate students currently receiving treatment for a bipolar diagnosis
• Scholarship Amount: $250-$2,300

180 MEDICAL SCHOLARSHIP PROGRAM

• Who Is Eligible: Full-time students in two-year, four-year, or graduate programs under care for a spinal cord injury, spina bifida, transverse myelitis, bladder exstrophy, neurogenic bladder, or ostomy
• Scholarship Amount: $1,000

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

CAN PEOPLE ON DISABILITY GET SUPPORT BY SUBMITTING THE FAFSA?

Students who receive SSDI or SSI can qualify for federal financial aid. Fill out the Free Application for Federal Student Aid (FAFSA) to see how much aid you qualify for. Any SSDI or SSI payments you receive will be taken into account when determining federal financial aid eligibility.

IS ADHD A DISABILITY?

The U.S. Department of Education’s
Office for Civil Rights considers
Attention Deficit Hyperactivity Disorder (ADHD) a disability, and students with ADHD may be entitled to testing accommodations at college. However, you may not qualify for SSDI payments unless your disability is both medically documented and impairs daily life
based on certain qualifications
.

DO STUDENTS WITH DISABILITIES QUALIFY FOR STUDENT LOAN FORGIVENESS?

If you are considered “totally and permanently disabled,” you can
qualify for student loan forgiveness
for William D. Ford Federal Direct Loan (Direct Loan) Program loans, Federal Family Education Loan (FFEL) Program loans, and Federal Perkins Loans. You may also be relieved of your service obligations for the TEACH Grant.

CAN STUDENTS WITH DISABILITIES GO TO COLLEGE FOR FREE?

While most schools do not offer free tuition specifically for students with disabilities, there are many options to help lower your tuition costs. For example, if you’re a student with financial need, many schools across the country
offer free tuition
for students from families making under a certain income threshold. If you’re a veteran with a disability from your time in service, you may also qualify for
scholarships specifically for veterans
.
